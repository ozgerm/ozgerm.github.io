Mustafa Özger

Assistant Professor, Aalborg University · Researcher, KTH Royal Institute of Technology

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronic Systems at Aalborg University, Copenhagen, and a Researcher at KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm. My research sits at the intersection of future telecommunication systems and machine learning for engineering — combining communication-theoretic reasoning with data-driven learning to design robust, uncertainty-aware methods for safety-critical and resource-constrained environments.

A cross-cutting thread in my work is the use of information-theoretic reasoning to guide learning: how channel structure, coding principles, and measures of informativeness can improve model selection, dataset design, and reliable inference in engineering systems.

About

My work spans wireless and networked systems, 5G/6G and non-terrestrial networks, AI-native network management, mobility management, localisation, uncertainty-aware inference, and system-level optimisation. I am interested in when learning-based methods can be made robust, data-efficient, and trustworthy enough to operate inside real communication infrastructure, and in how information-theoretic principles can make those methods more reliable under data scarcity and distributional shift.

I have experience in industrially motivated projects and multi-partner research coordination, with applications spanning communications, defence-relevant autonomous systems, and cyber-physical infrastructures. I teach and supervise across BSc, MSc, PhD, and postdoctoral levels.

Research themes

Machine learning for engineering systems

Reinforcement learning, multi-agent and hierarchical RL, data-driven control, uncertainty-aware modelling, explainability, and domain-grounded learning for complex engineering systems.

Information-theoretic learning and uncertainty

Information-theoretic measures for model selection and dataset design, channel and signal classification, representation learning for physical-layer data, and reliable inference under data scarcity and distributional shift.

Communication systems and networked engineering

Wireless networks, non-terrestrial networks, resource allocation, mobility management, URLLC, localisation, sensing, and signal processing.

System-level networking and management

Reliability, traffic management, network architecture, and AI-assisted resource allocation and control across terrestrial, aerial, and non-terrestrial systems.

Industrial and collaborative research

Project leadership and collaboration with Airbus, Ericsson, Deutsche Telekom, Turkcell, Viavi, and other stakeholders through national and international consortia.

Selected contributions
  • Learning-based and model-based methods for mobility management and service availability in cellular-connected UAV systems, establishing when machine learning delivers reliable operational gains over analytical control design.
  • Multi-agent and hierarchical deep reinforcement learning for communication-system control — handover management, dynamic cluster reconfiguration, power allocation, and trajectory-aware optimisation.
  • Information-theoretic and ML methods for engineering inference and representation, including channel classification via Hankelization, learnability-oriented dataset clustering, denoising-autoencoder methods, AI-assisted localisation, and uncertainty-aware decision support.
  • Reliability meets learning for safety-critical communications: risk-aware resource allocation for URLLC and dependability-oriented AI-native network design.
  • Combining communication-theoretic modelling with machine learning across wireless sensing, non-terrestrial networks, spectrum sharing, and intelligent network management.
